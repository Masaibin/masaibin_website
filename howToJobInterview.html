Job Interview Tips

Job Interview Tips

Job interviews can be stressful. Explore our tips and tricks for some additional preparation for your interview.

No matter who you are, you may have already been through a job interview or are about to go through one. Job interviews are not as stressful as you assume, follow these simple tips to ensure you perform your best!

Appearance

First impressions can either make or break your entire interview. The very first aspect of you your employer notices is your appearance. No wants to fail their interview before it even started. A few tips to keep in mind:

  1. No matter what your job is, wear a suit and tie. Look as professional as you can be.
  2. Tattoos, piercings, and other extremities should be covered up or removed. At the very least, make sure they cannot be seen by your interviewer.
  3. Wear a smile on your face, keep your back straight, and be happy to be there. Body language is used by your interviewer to quickly determine your overall mood and enthusiasm. Remember you are happy to be there for a life-changing opportunity.

Speech

Speech is merely your greetings and formal introductions. The actual interview is mostly your interviewer asking questions about yourself, why you want the job, etc. We will get to the actual interview next, but also look at these tips as well:

  1. Use formal language. Do not use slang such as “what’s up?” or “hey, dude!” to greet your interview. A simple “Hello” could do. For extra measure, you may ask “How are you?” after your greeting to show off a politer mood.
  2. Don’t make your interviewer have to ask all the questions. Try to answer standard questions such as your name or what position you’re applying for before your interviewer has to ask them. This is not always necessary, but a great way to have something to say.
  3. Speech is not limited to your words, it can be shown through your body language as well. Shake your interviewer’s hand firmly while greeting him. Take a seat comfortable and sit with your back straight.

The Interview

The interview itself. As said before, this is where your interviewer asks you questions about yourself to evaluate whether you are capable of the job or not. There are no right answers for this, but there are some guidelines to ensure you perform your best:

  1. Speak calmly and clearly. Your interviewer can get frustrated if he/she cannot hear what you are saying, which may lead to a less successful chance of being hired.
  2. Answer all your questions truthfully. No matter the question, do not lie. Lies will catch up to you, no matter what. As soon as your lie has been detected, you will risk getting fired.
  3. This cannot be stressed enough: stay calm. Keeping your cool is most important as it shows your confidence and integrity to your employer. No one wants to hire a quiet, indecisive person.

Conclusion

In the end, it is all up to you to make your interview a success. There is never going to be only one right answer to anything in life, you have to do your part as well. We at Masaibin hope you get your dream job!
