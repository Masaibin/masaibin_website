Writing your Resume

How to Write a Resume

There are many types of resumes. This guide builds on the chronological type. For other resume styles, click here

See a longer list of resume styles at "The Balance"

One of the first things that should be done when developing your resume is choosing a font and font size. Ensure the font is legible and professional and leaves whitespace on the page. Additionally, make sure that the font size is not too big, to prevent overcrowding the page, and not too small, so it can be legible. Keep use of styling features to a minimum, and reserve them for headings or important aspects on the page.

The next step is to review some resume examples, which can be found here. These resume examples should be able to give you an idea of what the finished product should look like. You may also want to use a resume template. These can help give you a starting point, as well as some ideas. You can add your information to the template, and then tweak it and edit it to your liking.

Resume keywords can also be an important part of your resume. Companies will often use special software to “screen” resumes, searching for certain keywords. These can be incorporated into skills, special abilities, and experiences, which can also help hiring managers to see how you can contribute to their company.

Getting advice on writing your resume can also be helpful. Writing a resume is not easy, and getting advice can help reduce the load. Some resume writing advice can be found here.

Lastly, you must proof-read your resume. Look out for grammer and spelling errors, as well as any inconsistancies, be it in syling or information. You may also want to have a friend or family memebr read your resume. There is also grammer-chcking software, sich as Grammarly, that can help catch small errors. However, you would not want to rely soley on software.

The first step in filling out a job application form is to review your employment history. This can be done by simply listing your history of employment, education, experiences, and job title. You can also use your resume to help you list this information.

Similar to the resume, many sample job application forms are available, and one can practice filling those out. You can find some sample applications here.

There are many types of job applications, including in-person and online forms.

When applying in person

When applying in person, be sure to ask for a job application form to take home and fill out. When you complete it at home, be sure to take your time, so it will look neat, complete, and professional, as well as contain precise and accurate information when you submit it. Additionally, be sure to dress professionally, when going to pick it up and drop it off, as you may end up speaking to someone of high authority at the company. This may include a manager, director, etc. It is also possible to be interviewed on the spot, so you will want to dress appropriately.

When applying online

Many companies have started to use online software for submitting job applications. When filling out an online job application, you will want to be sure to follow all of the provided directions, to ensure you are entering the required information. When you complete the form, review it thoroughly, as you would do with an in-person form. Then follow the instructions to submit your application.

Job applications vary greatly but may include information such as:

Personal Information:

  • Name
  • Address
  • City, State, Zip Code
  • Phone Number
  • Criminal Record

Education:

  • Schools/Colleges Attended
  • Major
  • Degree/Diploma
  • Graduation Date

Information about the Position you are Applying for:

  • Title of the job you are applying for
  • Hours/days available to work
  • When you can start work

Employment Information:

  • Names, addresses, phone numbers, and other information of previous employers
  • Supervisor’s name
  • Dates of employment
  • Salary

References

  • List of references, often three or more – names, job title or relationship, addresses, phone numbers

Resume

  • List your resume

 

Writing Your Cover Letter

How to write a Cover Letter

A cover letter is an important part of the job application process. It is typically a document sent with your resume, in order to provide additional information on your experiences and skills.

When writing your cover letter, keep in mind that you should not simply repeat what is written on your resume. Instead, include additional information, such as why you think you are a good match for the job in question. Your cover letter is similar to a sales pitch, in that it provides credentials and can help you get an interview for the job.

There are many types of cover letters. However, these guidelines are general and can be adapted to fit each type.

Your cover letter should be an “add-on” to your resume. Not a duplicate. There are several differences between the two. Your cover letter should be unique. It is typically the first thing an employer sees, so it will create a first impression. If it has typos or is generic with no specific references, it will likely be rejected.

An effective cover letter is error-free and explains why you are interested in a particular job, as well as skills and experiences that apply to the job. However, there is also information that you do not need to include in your cover letter. This may include personal information, questions, and more. Do not write too much. Your letter must be focused and concise.

You should not be afraid to customize your cover letter. Cover letters should be customized to include the following, though it is not a complete list. For more information on what to include in a cover letter, click here.

  • Which job you’re applying for (Make sure to include the job title in your opening paragraph)
  • How you learned about or found the job
  • Why you think you are qualified for the job
  • What you have to offer the employer, and why you want to work at their company
  • A thank you for being considered for the job

A cover letter should also include the following sections:

  • A header
  • You contact information
  • A salutation
  • An introduction
  • A body, typically one or two paragraphs
  • A closing section
  • And a signature
